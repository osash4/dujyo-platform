# Basic Compliance Framework - Dujyo Entertainment Platform

**Last Updated: January 15, 2024**

## 1. Overview

This document outlines the basic compliance framework for Dujyo Entertainment Platform, covering essential legal and regulatory requirements for operating a Web3 entertainment platform with cryptocurrency and NFT features.

## 2. Regulatory Compliance

### 2.1 Financial Services Compliance

**Anti-Money Laundering (AML)**
- Transaction monitoring and reporting
- Suspicious activity detection
- Customer due diligence (CDD)
- Enhanced due diligence (EDD) for high-risk users
- Record keeping for 5 years
- Staff training on AML procedures

**Know Your Customer (KYC)**
- Identity verification for users
- Address verification
- Sanctions screening
- Politically Exposed Person (PEP) screening
- Ongoing monitoring of customer relationships
- Risk-based approach to verification

**Counter-Terrorism Financing (CTF)**
- Sanctions list screening
- Suspicious transaction reporting
- Risk assessment procedures
- Staff training on CTF requirements
- Cooperation with law enforcement

### 2.2 Securities Compliance

**Token Classification**
- DYO tokens are utility tokens, not securities
- Clear documentation of token utility
- No investment promises or guarantees
- Transparent tokenomics and distribution
- Regular legal review of token status

**Investment Advice Disclaimer**
- No financial advice provided
- Educational content only
- Risk warnings for all investments
- User responsibility for investment decisions
- Clear disclaimers on all materials

### 2.3 Tax Compliance

**User Tax Obligations**
- Clear information about tax implications
- Transaction reporting capabilities
- Educational resources on tax obligations
- No tax advice provided
- User responsibility for compliance

**Platform Tax Obligations**
- Corporate tax compliance
- Sales tax where applicable
- International tax obligations
- Transfer pricing compliance
- Regular tax reporting

## 3. Data Protection Compliance

### 3.1 General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)

**Lawful Basis for Processing**
- Consent for marketing communications
- Contract for service provision
- Legal obligation for compliance
- Legitimate interest for security and analytics

**Data Subject Rights**
- Right to access personal data
- Right to rectification of inaccurate data
- Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten")
- Right to restrict processing
- Right to data portability
- Right to object to processing

**Data Protection Measures**
- Privacy by design and default
- Data minimization and purpose limitation
- Regular privacy impact assessments
- Data protection officer (DPO) appointment
- Staff training on data protection

### 3.2 California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA)

**Consumer Rights**
- Right to know about personal information
- Right to delete personal information
- Right to opt-out of sale of personal information
- Right to non-discrimination

**Business Obligations**
- Privacy policy requirements
- Consumer request handling
- Data inventory and mapping
- Third-party service provider agreements
- Employee training on CCPA

### 3.3 Other Privacy Laws

**Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA)**
- No collection from children under 13
- Parental consent for users under 18
- Special protections for children's data
- Clear age verification procedures

**Other Jurisdictions**
- Compliance with local privacy laws
- Data localization requirements
- Cross-border data transfer restrictions
- Local data protection authority requirements
